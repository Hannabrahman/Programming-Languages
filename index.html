Singular Value Decomposition (SVD)

Matrix Decomposition Algorithm

Singular value decomposition (SVD) is a factorization of a rectangular matrix A into three matrices U, S and V. The two matrices U and Vare orthogonal matrices while S (Singular value) is a diagonal matrix. The factorization means that we can multiply the three matrices to get back the original matrix A = USV'. The transpose matrix is obtained through A' = U'SV .

Since both orthogonal matrix and diagonal matrix have many nice properties, SVD is one of the most powerful matrix decomposition that used in many applications such as least square (regression), feature selection (PCA, MDS), spectral clustering, image restoration and 3D computer vision (Fundamental matrix estimation), equilibrium of Markov Chain,  and many others.

Matrices Uand V are not unique, their columns come from the concatenation of eigenvectors of symmetric matrices AA' and A'A. Since eigenvectors of symmetric matrix are orthogonal (and linearly independent), they can be used as basis vectors (coordinate system) to span a multidimensional space. The absolute value of the determinant of orthogonal matrix is one, thus the matrix always has inverse. Furthermore, each column (and each row) of orthogonal matrix has unit norm

The diagonal matrix S contains the square of eigenvalues of symmetric matrix A'A. The diagonal elements are non-negative numbers and they are called singular values. Because they come from a symmetric matrix, the eigenvalues (and the eigenvectors) are all real numbers (no complex numbers).
